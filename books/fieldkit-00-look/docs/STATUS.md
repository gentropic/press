# STATUS — Field Kit No.00 ("Look!")

_Last updated: 2026-06-07._

## Where we are

**Concept stage.** Greenlit after the No.01 reader review (`specs/AUDIENCE.md`).
Premise, audience, and the seed image (Tante Christel's road-cut soil layers) are
set. No generator, no pages yet — the content design is the next real work.

## The creative work to do (ordered)

1. **Page sequence / storyboard.** Decide the ~20–30 spreads as concrete
   observation beats, NOT a curriculum. Candidate arc:
   _look at the hill → it has layers (the road-cut) → the layers are different →
   it's different over here than over there → pick up two stones, compare →
   dig a little hole, what changed? → the ground keeps secrets you can see →
   (bridge) "next: how grown-ups guess what they can't see" → No.01._
2. **Lower the gloss register.** Keep the two-voice device, but the `//` line names
   a *habit of mind* ("// notice how it changes"), not a technical term. Draft the
   read-aloud + gloss pair for each spread.
3. **"Try it" prompts.** At least a few spreads should ask the child to DO
   something real (compare two rocks, dig, draw). The GCU "propose experiences"
   spine, age-appropriate.
4. **Art plan.** Reuse Kit's palette/scaffold; figures should be concrete and
   sensory (a real road-cut, real stones), not diagrams/plots. Decide if it's still
   seed-driven or hand-composed (observation may want hand-composed scenes).
5. **Generator** (`look.py` or similar) → `\usepackage{kit}`, shared fonts, the
   house `build.sh`. Build last, once the content exists.
6. Front matter per `specs/FRONTMATTER.md` (reserve ficha if registering).

## Open decisions

- **Trim/size:** match No.01 (square 210×210) for series consistency, or a smaller/
  sturdier format for little hands? (series-coherence vs. ergonomics)
- **Seed-driven vs. hand-composed art:** No.01's fields are simulated from seed 7;
  No.00's observation scenes may be better hand-composed. Decide early — it changes
  the generator's nature.
- **How explicit is the bridge to No.01?** A closing "next book" nod, or stand fully
  alone?
